I help people see through complex information using visual and interactive means.

I believe ensuring information transparency for decision-making is (and will be) one of the biggest challenges of the data-driven digital age. Novel data science methods and techniques need to be developed and evaluated for making data accessible and interpretable to people, regardless of their training and background.

My research interests directed towards this goal lie at the cross-cutting areas of information visualization, visual analytics, and human-computer interaction.


Background
Currently at PNNL, I have the pleasure of collaborating with experts from diverse backgrounds such as biology, climate science, machine learning, cyber security, etc.
In my previous roles as a graduate student and a postdoctoral researcher, I have had the good fortune of being mentored by some of the most prominent thought leaders of our community. Dr. Robert Kosara was my PhD advisor at UNC Charlotte with whom I worked on my dissertation on quantifiable methods for bridging human perception with visual pattern search.
Prof. Claudio Silva and Prof. Enrico Bertini were my postdoc mentors at New York University with whom I worked on applying visual analytics techniques for solving domain experts' problems as part of the the NSF funded DataONE initiative.

Research Areas
Scalable Visual Comparison Methods
Let domain scientists reason about model similarity and help them select the most accurate models by interactively comparing multiple facets of model performance.
[EuroVis14]   [TVCG14]   [CISE15]

Model Explainability and Trust
Provide domain experts and model developers with tools that explain the decisions of machine learning models and help them semantically validate models.
[HILDA17]   [TVCG17]   [VAST17]

High-Dimensional Pattern Search
Provide guidance to analysts for finding patterns in high-dimensional subspaces by devising metrics that quantify salient patterns.
[InfoVis10]   [LDAV12]   [CGF2015]   [LDAV2016]

  Research Areas
Studies on Visualization Usage "in the wild"
Conduct user studies with experts from biology and climate science domains to evaluate if and how optimal visualization design can overcome potential biases due to familiarity.
[TVCG16]   [CHI17]   [TVCG18 (under review)]

Visualization Perception & Design Analysis
Study and survey of the visualization design space for devising classification schemes that bridge human perception with visual encodings.
[TVCG15]   [CGF17]

Privacy-Preserving Visualization
Adapt visualizations to prevent disclosure of sensitive information by developing information loss metrics that can help address the trade-off between privacy gain and loss of utility due to anonymization.
[InfoVis11]   [CGF12]   [CGF13]   [EHRVis14]
